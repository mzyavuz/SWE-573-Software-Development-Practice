## 1. Introduction

### 1.1 What is The Hive?

The Hive is a community-based TimeBank platform that enables neighbors to exchange skills and services using time as currency instead of money. Every hour of service is valued equally - whether you're teaching guitar, helping with gardening, or providing tech support, one hour equals one time credit.

### 1.2 How Does It Work?

**The Basic Concept:**
- You join the community and receive 1 hour of time credit to start
- Offer services you can provide (Offers)
- Request services you need (Needs)
- Exchange time credits when services are completed
- Build community connections while helping each other

**Key Features:**
- üó∫Ô∏è **Map-Based Discovery**: Find services near you on an interactive map
- ‚è∞ **TimeBank Balance**: Track your time credits (10-hour maximum)
- üìÖ **Schedule Proposals**: Coordinate service timing with balance validation
- üí¨ **Private Messaging**: Communicate directly with matched users
- ‚≠ê **Ratings & Reviews**: Build trust through community feedback
- üè∑Ô∏è **Semantic Tags**: Categorize and discover services easily

### 1.3 Who Can Use The Hive?

Anyone in your community! Whether you're:
- A student offering tutoring in exchange for help moving
- A retiree sharing art history knowledge for tech support
- A parent needing babysitting in exchange for proofreading
- A teen running errands to earn tutoring hours

## 3. Understanding the TimeBank System

### 3.1 What Are Time Credits?

Time credits (also called TimeBank Hours) are the currency of The Hive platform. They represent hours of service given or received.

**Key Principles:**
- ‚úÖ 1 hour of any service = 1 time credit
- ‚úÖ All skills are valued equally
- ‚úÖ Credits are earned by providing services
- ‚úÖ Credits are spent by receiving services

### 3.2 Your Initial Balance

When you join The Hive, you receive:
- **1 hour of time credit** to start
- This allows you to request a service right away
- Or you can provide a service first to build your balance

### 3.3 Balance Rules

**Minimum Balance:**
- Can be 0 hours (but you can't request services)
- Can never go negative

**Maximum Balance:**
- **10 hours maximum** per user
- This ensures active participation and fairness
- Once you reach 10 hours, you must use credits before earning more

### 3.4 Checking Your Balance

Your balance is always visible:
1. Top right corner of every page
2. Dashboard balance widget
3. Profile page

**Balance Display Shows:**
- Current balance (e.g., "7 hours")
- Pending schedule proposals 

### 3.5 How Credits Transfer

**When You Provide a Service (Provider):**
1. Complete the service
2. Consumer confirms completion
3. Credits transfer from Consumer to Provider
4. Your balance increases

**When You Receive a Service (Consumer):**
1. Accept a schedule proposal (credits reserved)
2. Service is completed
3. You confirm completion
4. Credits transfer to Provider
5. Your balance decreases

**Important:** Credits only transfer after **mutual confirmation** of service completion.

### 3.6 Balance Warnings

The system will warn you if:
- ‚ö†Ô∏è Your balance is insufficient for a service request
- ‚ö†Ô∏è Accepting a proposal would exceed the provider's 10-hour cap
- ‚ö†Ô∏è You're approaching your maximum balance

## 9. Schedule Proposals

### 9.1 What Are Schedule Proposals?

Schedule proposals let you suggest specific dates and times for service delivery. The system validates that both parties have sufficient balance before allowing acceptance.

### 9.2 Creating a Schedule Proposal

**Either the Consumer or Provider can propose:**

1. Go to the matched service (in "My Services")
2. Click **"Propose Schedule"** button
3. Fill in proposal form:

**Proposal Form Fields:**

**Proposed Date:**
```
Example: 2025-12-25
```

**Proposed Time:**
```
Example: 14:00 (2:00 PM)
```

**Duration (in hours):**
```
Example: 2
(Must match service's estimated hours)
```

**Additional Notes:**
```
Example: "I can be flexible with the exact start time if 
2 PM doesn't work. Please bring your own guitar. Looking 
forward to it!"
```

4. Click **"Send Proposal"**
5. The other party receives a notification

### 9.3 Balance Validation

**Before accepting a proposal, the system checks:**

**Consumer (Person Receiving Service):**
- ‚úÖ Must have enough hours in balance
- ‚ùå "Insufficient balance" error if not enough

**Provider (Person Giving Service):**
- ‚úÖ Receiving hours must not exceed 10-hour maximum
- ‚ùå "Would exceed maximum balance" error if over limit

**Example Validation:**
```
Consumer has 3 hours
Provider has 9 hours
Service is 2 hours

Consumer can accept: ‚úÖ (3 - 2 = 1 hour remaining)
Provider can accept: ‚ùå (9 + 2 = 11 hours, exceeds cap)

Solution: Provider must use some hours first
```

### 9.4 Responding to Schedule Proposals

When you receive a proposal:

1. Check your notifications or messages
2. View the proposal details
3. Choose to:

**Accept the Proposal:**
- Click **"Accept Proposal"**
- System validates balances
- If valid:
  - Service status ‚Üí "Scheduled"
  - Hours reserved from consumer's balance
  - Both parties notified
  - Service ready to start on scheduled date

**Reject the Proposal:**
- Click **"Reject Proposal"**
- Optionally provide a reason
- Suggest an alternative time in messages


### 9.5 Viewing Pending Proposals

To see all your schedule proposals:

1. Go to **"My Services"**
2. Click on a matched service
3. See **"Schedule Proposals"** section showing:
   - Date and time proposed
   - Who proposed it
   - Status (Pending/Accepted/Rejected)
   - Location (Optional)
   - Action buttons

### 9.6 What Happens After Acceptance?

Once a schedule proposal is accepted:

‚úÖ **Service status**: "Scheduled"  
‚úÖ **Consumer balance**: Hours reserved (shows as "pending")  
‚úÖ **Both parties notified**: Confirmation messages sent

## 10. Service Execution

### 10.1 Service Lifecycle

Services go through these stages:

1. **Open**: Accepting applications
2. **Selected**: Provider chosen, scheduling in progress
3. **Scheduled**: Date/time confirmed, hours reserved
4. **In Progress**: Service has started (dual confirmation)
5. **Completion**: Awaiting completion confirmation 
6. **Completed**: Service finished, hours transferred

### 10.2 Starting the Service

On the scheduled date, **both parties must confirm start:**

**Step 1: Provider Confirms Start**

1. Provider goes to the service page
2. Clicks **"Start Service"** button
3. Status shows "Waiting for Consumer confirmation"

**Step 2: Consumer Confirms Start**

1. Consumer goes to the service page
2. Clicks **"Start Service"** button
3. Once both confirm, status changes to **"In Progress"**

**Why Dual Confirmation?**
- Prevents misunderstandings about timing
- Ensures both parties agree service has begun
- Creates clear record for dispute resolution

### 10.3 During the Service

While the service is in progress:

**Provider:**
- Deliver the service as described
- Communicate any issues immediately
- Be professional and punctual

**Consumer:**
- Be available and prepared
- Provide necessary materials/information
- Be respectful and cooperative

**If Problems Arise:**
- Use private messages to communicate
- Try to resolve issues directly
- If needed, contact platform support

### 10.4 Completing the Service

When the service is finished, **both parties must confirm completion:**

**Step 1: Provider Marks Complete**

1. Go to the service page
2. Click **"Mark as Complete"** button
3. Optionally add completion notes
4. System notifies Consumer

**Step 2: Consumer Confirms Completion**

1. Receive notification that Provider marked complete
2. Review that service was satisfactory
3. Go to service page
4. Click **"Confirm Completion"** button

**Step 3: Credit Transfer**

Once both confirm:
- ‚úÖ Hours transfer from Consumer to Provider
- ‚úÖ Balances updated immediately
- ‚úÖ Service status ‚Üí "Completed"
- ‚úÖ Both parties can now leave reviews
- ‚úÖ Survey forms appear


### 10.6 Completion Survey

After service completion, both parties receive a survey:

**Survey Questions:**
- Was the service completed as described?
- How many hours were actually needed?
- Was the time estimate accurate?
- Any additional feedback?

**Purpose:**
- Improve service descriptions
- Help future users make informed decisions
- Provide platform insights for improvements

## 13. Troubleshooting

### 13.1 Login Issues

**Problem: "Invalid email or password" error**

**Solutions:**
1. Double-check email address for typos
2. Verify caps lock is off for password
3. Try "Forgot Password" to reset
4. Ensure account is verified (check verification email)
5. Clear browser cache and cookies

**Problem: "Email not verified" message**

**Solutions:**
1. Check your email inbox for verification link
2. Check spam/junk folder
3. Click "Resend Verification Email"
4. Use correct email address
5. Verification link expires after 24 hours

**Problem: Can't receive verification email**

**Solutions:**
1. Check spam/junk/promotions folders
2. Add no-reply@thehive.com to contacts
3. Verify email address is correct
4. Try different email provider
5. Contact support if issue persists

### 13.2 Service Creation Issues

**Problem: Can't publish service**

**Solutions:**
1. Fill in all required fields (marked with *)
2. Check that description is at least 50 characters
3. Add at least one tag
4. Verify estimated hours is between 1-3
5. Check location field is filled (if in-person)

**Problem: Tags not appearing**

**Solutions:**
1. Type tag name slowly (suggestions take 1-2 seconds)
2. Press Enter to create new tag
3. Check internet connection
4. Try refreshing the page
5. Use existing suggested tags

### 13.3 Application Issues

**Problem: "Apply" button is grayed out**

**Possible reasons:**
- You're not logged in (login first)
- You already applied (check "My Applications")
- You own this service (can't apply to your own)
- Service is no longer open (already assigned)
- Service was deleted or expired

**Problem: Can't withdraw application**

**Solutions:**
- Refresh the page
- Check if application was already accepted (can't withdraw after acceptance)
- Try using a different browser
- Clear browser cache

### 13.4 Schedule Proposal Issues

**Problem: "Insufficient balance" error**

**Cause:** Consumer doesn't have enough hours

**Solutions:**
1. Check your current balance
2. Complete a service to earn more hours
3. Wait for pending credits to transfer
4. Request a service with fewer hours

**Problem: "Would exceed maximum balance" error**

**Cause:** Provider would exceed 10-hour cap

**Solutions:**
1. Provider should use some hours first
2. Request a service as Consumer
3. Wait until balance is lower
4. Schedule service for later date

**Problem: Can't create schedule proposal**

**Solutions:**
1. Ensure service has been matched (application accepted)
2. Check that no schedule is already accepted
3. Verify you're either Consumer or Provider
4. Fill in all required fields
5. Ensure date is in the future

### 13.5 Messaging Issues

**Problem: Messages not sending**

**Solutions:**
1. Check internet connection
2. Refresh the page
3. Verify message isn't too long (1000 char limit)
4. Check that thread is still active
5. Try different browser

**Problem: Can't see message thread**

**Solutions:**
1. Ensure application has been accepted (messages only available after matching)
2. Check "My Services" ‚Üí find matched service ‚Üí click "Messages"
3. Verify service hasn't been completed and archived
4. Refresh the page

### 13.6 Balance Issues

**Problem: Balance not updating after completion**

**Solutions:**
1. Ensure both parties confirmed completion
2. Check "Transaction History" for pending transfers
3. Wait a few minutes and refresh
4. Verify service status is "Completed"
5. Contact support if persists after 24 hours

**Problem: Balance shows wrong amount**

**Solutions:**
1. Hard refresh (Ctrl+F5 or Cmd+Shift+R)
2. Clear browser cache
3. Log out and log back in
4. Check transaction history for discrepancies
5. Contact support with transaction details

### 13.7 Map and Search Issues

**Problem: Map not loading**

**Solutions:**
1. Enable location services in browser
2. Check internet connection
3. Allow location permission when prompted
4. Refresh the page
5. Try different browser
6. Use List View as alternative

**Problem: No services showing on map**

**Solutions:**
1. Zoom out to see wider area
2. Remove or adjust filters
3. Check that services exist in your area
4. Verify service type filter (Offers/Needs/All)
5. Try search function instead

## 14. Frequently Asked Questions

### 14.1 Getting Started

**Q: How much does it cost to join The Hive?**  
A: The Hive is completely free! No money changes hands - only time credits.

**Q: Do I need to provide a service before requesting one?**  
A: No! You start with 1 free hour that you can use right away. You can either provide a service first or use your free hour to receive a service.

**Q: What if I don't have any special skills?**  
A: Everyone has something to offer! Simple tasks like errands, moving help, walking dogs, or listening ears are valuable to others.

**Q: How do I know if someone is trustworthy?**  
A: Check their profile for:
- Average rating from other users
- Reviews describing their past services
- Number of completed services
- How long they've been a member

---

### 14.2 Time Credits

**Q: Why is there a 10-hour maximum balance?**  
A: The cap ensures:
- Active participation (use your hours!)
- Fair distribution across community
- Prevents hoarding
- Encourages ongoing exchanges

**Q: What happens if I reach the 10-hour cap?**  
A: You must use some hours before earning more. Request a service you need!

**Q: Can time credits expire?**  
A: No! Your credits never expire as long as your account is active.

**Q: What if someone doesn't show up for a scheduled service?**  
A: Your reserved hours are released back to your balance. You can report the issue to admins if it happens repeatedly.

**Q: Are all services worth the same time credits?**  
A: Yes! One hour of any service equals one time credit. A software developer's hour equals a gardener's hour.

---

### 14.3 Services

**Q: Can I edit a service after posting?**  
A: Yes, as long as no one has been matched yet. After accepting an applicant, some fields become locked.

**Q: How long do services stay active?**  
A: Services remain active until:
- You delete them
- You complete the service
- An admin removes them (for violations)

**Q: Can I post the same service multiple times?**  
A: Yes! If you want to help multiple people with the same skill, create separate service posts for each.

**Q: What's the difference between Offer and Need?**  
A: 
- **Offer** = You provide service, you earn hours
- **Need** = You receive service, you spend hours

**Q: Can I apply to my own service?**  
A: No, that's not allowed. You can't apply to services you created.

---

### 14.4 Applications

**Q: How many people can I accept for one service?**  
A: Only ONE. Once you accept someone, other applications are automatically rejected.

**Q: Can I un-accept someone?**  
A: Not easily. Once accepted, you're committed. Only cancel if absolutely necessary, and communicate clearly with the other person.

**Q: Why was my application rejected?**  
A: Service owners can choose anyone for various reasons:
- Availability mismatch
- Lack of experience
- Another applicant was better fit
- Location distance
Don't take it personally!

**Q: How long should I wait before applying to more services?**  
A: You can apply to multiple services at once! Just don't over-commit yourself.

---

### 14.5 Schedule & Completion

**Q: What if we need to reschedule?**  
A: Either party can create a new schedule proposal. Discuss via messages first.

**Q: What happens if I can't complete a service?**  
A: Communicate immediately with the other person. Try to reschedule or find a replacement. Cancelling without notice harms your reputation.

**Q: Why do both people need to confirm start and completion?**  
A: Dual confirmation:
- Prevents disputes
- Creates clear record
- Ensures mutual agreement
- Protects both parties

**Q: What is the 48-hour auto-confirm?**  
A: If Consumer doesn't confirm completion within 48 hours of Provider marking complete, the system automatically confirms and transfers credits. This protects Providers from non-responsive Consumers.

---

### 14.6 Safety & Privacy

**Q: Is my personal information safe?**  
A: Yes! We only share:
- Name
- Profile photo
- Biography
- Rating
Your email, phone, date of birth, and exact balance are private.

**Q: What if I feel unsafe meeting someone?**  
A: 
- Meet in public places first
- Bring a friend to first meeting
- Tell someone where you're going
- Trust your instincts
- Report suspicious behavior

**Q: Can I report someone?**  
A: Yes! Use the "Report" button on services, messages, or reviews. Admins review within 48 hours.

**Q: What happens to reported users?**  
A: Depending on severity:
- Warning
- Temporary suspension
- Permanent ban
- Service removal

---

### 14.7 Technical

**Q: Do I need to download an app?**  
A: No! The Hive is a web-based platform. Access it through your web browser on any device.

**Q: Does The Hive work on mobile?**  
A: Yes, though mobile experience isn't fully optimized. Desktop/laptop recommended for best experience.

**Q: Can I use The Hive offline?**  
A: No, internet connection required for all features.

**Q: Which browsers are supported?**  
A: Chrome 90+, Firefox 88+, Safari 14+, Edge 90+ (all latest versions recommended)

**Q: Why aren't I receiving email notifications?**  
A: Email notifications are not yet implemented. Use in-app notifications (bell icon). Email notifications planned for v1.1 release.

## Appendix A: Glossary

**Balance:** The number of time credits (hours) you currently have available.

**Consumer:** A user who receives a service and spends time credits.

**Need:** A service request where you need someone's help.

**Offer:** A service you're willing to provide to others.

**Provider:** A user who delivers a service and earns time credits.

**Schedule Proposal:** A suggested date and time for service delivery.

**Semantic Tag:** A category label used to describe and search for services.

**Service:** General term for either an Offer or Need.

**The Commons:** The community forum for discussions (in beta testing).

**TimeBank:** The platform's currency system where all exchanges are measured in hours.

**Time Credit:** The currency unit in the TimeBank system (measured in hours).
